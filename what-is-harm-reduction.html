WHAT IS HARM REDUCTION?
Harm Reduction emphasizes reducing the harms associated with drug use and is based on the belief that all people, regardless of whether they use drugs or not, deserve well-being and respect. Harm reduction can be put into action through a number of public health interventions, such as the distribution of naloxone and drug-testing kits or the establishment of overdose prevention sites. It is an empirically supported approach for reducing overdose deaths, disease, and other harms associated with drug use and is in opposition to previous approaches that involved criminalizing and stigmatizing substance use and substance use disorder.

If you want to reduce drug-related harms like overdoses, harm reduction is the most effective approach.
