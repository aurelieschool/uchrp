THE UCHICAGO HARM REDUCTION PROJECT (UCHRP)
is a student organization at the University of Chicago dedicated to organizing harm reduction and overdose prevention projects in Chicago.

Registered drug overdose prevention program with the Illinois Department of Health and Human Services.
FACTS
Overdoses are Preventable
​NARCAN (the brand name of Naloxone) Nasal Spray is a medicine designed to rapidly reverse opioid overdose. It is given out by hospitals, libraries, and community organizations in Illinois.

Fentanyl, a major cause of the surge of overdose deaths, can be detected in substances through the use of fentanyl test strips. These are also provided by government and harm reduction organizations.

Evidence shows that naloxone distribution is extremely effective at reducing overdose fatalities.
Chicago is Heavily Impacted by the Opioid Crisis
The prevalence of opioid overdose in Chicago and Cook County has spiked over the last few years. From January 2018 to December 2020, there were 4,283 opioid-related deaths in Cook County with nearly 82 percent of those deaths from June 6 through December 23, 2020 showing the presence of fentanyl in their systems.
– Chicago Department of Public Health

Each dot on the map above is one overdose death in 2021.
Harm Reduction Matters
The Chicago Recovery Alliance (CRA), one of the largest and oldest harm reduction organizations, works to provide harm reduction resources to substance users in Chicago.

Their efforts have paid off.

From 1996-2000 heroin-related overdose deaths in Cook County rose form 224 to 465. Once CRA was established in 2001, the trend reversed and overdose deaths began to steadily decline for seven-years straight, falling 44%.

​With the state of Chicago's current Opioid Crisis, harm reduction is needed now more than ever.
